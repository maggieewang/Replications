## Overview
This project is a replication of the IZA discussion paper "Importing in-equality: Immigration and the Top 1 percent" by Arun Advani, 
Felix Koenig, Lorenzo Pessina, and Andy Summers (2020). 

From the abstract: 
> "In this paper we study the contribution of migrants to the rise in UK top incomes. Using administrative data on the universe of UK 
taxpayers we show migrants are over-represented at the top of the income distribution, with migrants twice as prevalent in the top 0.1% 
as anywhere in the bottom 97%. These high incomes are predominantly from labour, rather than capital, and migrants are concentrated in 
only a handful of industries, predominately finance. Almost all (85%) of the growth in the UK top 1% income share over the past 20 years
can be attributed to migration."

I replicate the main results of the paper using the administrative tax data made available by the authors. I plot the income distribution 
of migrants in the UK (figure 1a in the paper), the proportion of migrants in the top decile of the income distribution (figure 1b), and 
the evolution of the migrant share of income at the top fractiles (1%, 0.1%, 0.01%, 0.001%) from 1997-2018 (figure 2c).
